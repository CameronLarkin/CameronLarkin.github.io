By: Cameron Larkin






Early Season Success



The Suns are fun to watch again! And it isn't even a joke. I have watched more Phoenix Suns games than I have 76er games, than I have watched Clippers games, than I have watched Lakers games. This team brings in ratings to the league.

In a time where there is so much speculation about who is going to make the finals, or who is going to win MVP, the Suns have been in a small spotlight with their hot start to the season. Compared to last seasons 4-20 start, they are already 11-13 in their first 24 contests. A special thing about their 11-13 is that they have lost 10 games by less than 10 points. The importance of this stat shows when looking at the pace of the league an how quickly 10 points can be scored. All that to say that if the suns had maybe 2-3 more minutes in each of those 10 games they could realistically be sitting at 21-3.

Now obviously they are not going to pull out every single one of those games out, so we will call it an even 16-8 (5 and 5 split). With a record like that, a team that finished last year 21-61, would be sitting at the 4th or 5th seed in the western conference. Needless to say the Suns organization made a plethera of great offseason moves to make this early season success a reality, and what should really excite Suns fans is the fact that their team may actually be looking on the upside for the first time in a very, very long time. .




New Aquisitions



    1) Monty Williams

The coach doesn't matter! They get paid to sub players in and out! You don't need a good coach to be a great team! And to those I say... Just sit down and be quiet!

How can someone sit there and tell me that the fact that the Suns have had SIX coaches in FIVE years has not affected their team in the win collumn. Monty Williams has already led the Suns to their best start in 6 years. The man simply has a winning formula. His defensive mind is something that the Suns desperatley needed; before Williams arrived in Phoenix, Devin Booker was bottom 20% in defensive efficency in eavh of his first five seasons. Monty Williams has found a way to will an offensive minded star to guard the opposing team's best player night in and night out.

The respect that the new Suns coach has garnered around the league is a testement to his supreme smarts and gameplan. In years past the suns have had a mentality that if the other team scores, they will just have to find a way to score on their possesion too. Monty has taught these young players if they get more stops than the other team, then their offense will speak for itself. Regardless of how the season ends for the Suns, the future is looking bright, and whoever it was in the Suns front office that suggested hiring Monty Williams should get an enormous raise. He is by far the best staff signing that happened this offseason.

    2) Ricky Rubio

Most fans look at ol' Ricky Rubio and immediatly look at the negatives, ie. he can't shoot or he can't defend. Now in years past these facts about Rubio have been what has been holding him back. With the Phoenix Suns The Spanish guard gets to do what he does best, distribute the ball. Distribute the rock to teamates who are primary scorers such as Devin and Kelly.

Now Ricky Rubio's scoring is nothing to just skip over. The man averages a solid 14 ppg. Players such as Al Horford (a former allstar), Mike Conly (a borderline allstar), Rudy Gobert (widley respected as one of the best centers in the league), Aaron Gordon (a rising star) all average about 14 ppg. The Suns don't even need Rubio to be one of their main scorers, but he continues to be their thrid option in this years games.

It is pointless to sit here and try to state Ricky Rubio's improtance to the Suns organization, rather to tell fans to watch him play and watch how many open teamates he finnds with his superior court vision.
